Crankshaft and Camshaft

After you remove the cylinder and pistons and the outer engine parts, you can then spilt apart the crank case-the two halves
that fit together to house the crankshaft and camshaft. The crankshaft is mounted in the upper half of the crankcase and the front
end attaches to the front side pulley. The back end attaches to the flywheel, a large gear with a diameter that fills up most of the
space on the back end of the crankcase. The flywheel attaches to the clutch plate, which attaches to teh transmission. The camshaft is
mounted inside the lower half of the crankcase. The crankshaft and the camshaft each have a gear on the front side that run together
to propel the engine. Inside the crankcase, the camshaft attaches with a gear that leads to the oil tube and then to the oil sump and the
oil screen. The front end of the camshaft attaches to the oil pump and the back end of the camshaft does not attach to any moving parts.
The camshaft must be completely submerged in oil. When the oil level is getting low, the oil light will come on. Enough oil must be added to
resubmerge the camshaft. If no oil is added, eventually your engine will stop running and cause damage. The two halves of the crankcase
are factory machined to fit together precisely and specifically. You can't mix and match halves from different crankcases. You can only
use the halves that were factory made to fit each other.

Opening the Crankcase

  1. Remove the oil strainer, oil pressure switch, and crank case nuts
  2. In the right half of the crankcase, use the retaining springs to hold the cam followers in place
  3. Break the seal between the crankcase halves with a rubber hammer. Do not use sharp tools to break the seal of the two halves because it will cause serious damage
  4. After the seal between the two halves is broken, remove the right half of the crankcase, the crankshaft oil seal and the camshaft end plug
  5. Lift out the camshaft and crankshaft
  6. Remove the cam followers, bearing shells, and the oil pressure relief valve
