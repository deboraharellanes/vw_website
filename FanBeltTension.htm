Checking the Fan Belt

If the belt is stretched out, or has frayed edges, cracks, grease or oil it should be replaced. New belts should be inspected regularly during the first 100 miles of use because new belts stretch slightly.

  1. Remove the nut from the generator pulley shaft.
  2. Remove the outer half of the generator pulley
  3. Fit the proper number of spacer washers between the halves of the pulley
  4. The belt will deflect approxiamtely .6 inches when it is adjusted correctly
  5. When the deflection is correct, reinstall the outer half of the generator pulley
  6. Insert all the left over washers between the pulley nut and the outer pulley half
  7. Tighten the pulley nut
