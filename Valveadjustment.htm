Tools Needed to Check Valve Clearance

  • .004 - .006 feeler gauge
  • 13 mm wrench
  • flat nose screw driver
  • 3/8 wratchet
  • 3/8 torch wrench
  • 13 mm deep well socket
  • 15 mm deep well socket
  • 3/8 3 inch socket extention
  • 3/8 6 inch socket extention
  • valve cover gasket

Checking Valve Clearance

  1. Remove the distrubuter cap. Rotate the engine until the rotor points to the cylinder #1 lead wire
  2. Remove cylinders #1 and #2 rocker arm covers
  3. Use the feeler guage to check the clearance between the adjusting screw and the valve stem
    • If the clearance is correct the feeler guage slides in moderately tight
    • If the clearance is not correct, loosen the lock nut and the adjusting screw
  4. After you tighten the lock nut, recheck the clearance, because when you tightened the lock nut you might have altered the adjustment
  5. Rotate the engine one-half revolution counter-clockwise until the distributer rotor points to the cylinder #1 lead wire.
  6. Repeat the adjustment process for cylinder #2
  7. Install the new valve cover gasket on cylinders #1 and #2
  8. Remove the valve rocker arm cover on cylinders #3 and #4
  9. Rotate the engine one-half revolution counter-clockwise until the distributer rotor points to the cylinder #3 lead wire
  10. Adjust the clearances on cylinder #3
  11. Rotate the engine one-half revolution counter-clockwise until the distributor rotor points to the cylinder #4 lead wire and adjust the clearances
  12. Replace the rocker arm cover of cylinders #3 and #4
  13. Clean the sealing surface and replace the gasket
  14. Replace the distributor cap
